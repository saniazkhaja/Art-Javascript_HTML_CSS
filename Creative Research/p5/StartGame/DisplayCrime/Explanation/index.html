This is some more info about how the factors affect crime!

Temperature:
The higher the temperature is the more crime there is. Higher tempertures also tend to lead to more violent crimes due to the part that it changes brain chemistry. Higher temperatures are associated with more burglaries and theft. The number of shooting victims also increases as the temperature increases. There was a 1.7% increase in criminal activity for each centigrade rise in temperature. Hot summer months have had more violent crime such as assault and murder.

Gun Ownership:
More gun ownership means there tends to be more firearm assault and firearm robbery. There also tends to be more violent crime since the guns are fatal.

Unemployment Rate:
There is more violent crime with a higher unemployment rate. Periods of long unemployment increases the crime rate. There tends to be more theft in such cases.

Population:
A more dense population in an area tends to lead to more crime. Cities tend to have more crime than suburbs and rural areas.

Poverty Level:
Stats have shown that poverty is related to crime. The more poverty in an area, the more crime there tends to be. Joblessness, less access to quality education, low employment and lack of oppurtunity is related to poverty. Some crimes offer people goods they may not be able to afford and these people feel they do not have much to loose which then increases crime. The type of crime is mainly theft.

Teens, 20s, 30s Aged Population:
Most crimes are commited by people in there teens, 20s and 30s, If there is a higher population with this age, there would also be a higher crime rate in the city. Seniors do not commit as much crime compared to younger people.

Precipitation:
Higher level of precipitation leads to fewer robberies and burglaries. It does not affect overall crime much.

Lack of Jobs:
